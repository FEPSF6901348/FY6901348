Time Crystals

Introduction

Time Crystals are a part of condensed matter physics. Time Crystals are a quantum system of particles whose lowest energy state is one in which particles are in repetitive motion. The system cannot lose energy to the environment and come to rest because it is already in its quantum ground state. Time Crystals are a relatively new and exotic phase of matter that was proposed by Nobel laureate Frank Wilczek in 2012 [1].

1.1 Time Translation Symmetry

Time translation symmetry (also known as temporal translation symmetry). It is a fundamental concept in physics and maths. It refers to the invariance of physical laws and phenomena over time. In other words, if a physical system exhibits time translation symmetry, it means that the laws governing that system remain unchanged as time progresses.

1.1.1 Conservation of Energy

Time translation symmetry is closely related to the conservation of energy. If the laws of physics do not change over time, it implies that the total energy of a closed physical system remains constant over time. This principle is known as the conservation of energy.

1.1.2 Mathematical Formulation and Broken Symmetry

Mathematically, time translation symmetry is often expressed using the concept of time derivatives. If the laws of physics are invariant under time translations, it means that the equations describing the system's behaviour remain the same when time is shifted by a constant value.

1.2 Quantum Computing

Quantum computers represent a completely new approach to computing. By using principles of quantum physics, they can solve very complex statistical problems that normal computers can't. Quantum Computing is built on quantum bits, or qubits, which can store zeros and ones simultaneously. This is known as superposition [2].

1.2.1 Time Crystals in Quantum Computing

Google's Quantum Computing team made headlines in 2019 when they performed the first-ever computation that ordinary computers weren't thought to be able to do in a practical amount of time. The time crystal demo marks one of the first times a Quantum Computer has found gainful employment [3].

1.3 'Recipe' of Time Crystals

A Time Crystal is a newly realised phase of matter in which particles move in a regular, repeating cycle without burning any energy. The phase arises through a combination of three special ingredients: Many-Body Localisation, Eigenstate Order, and a Periodic Driver.

1.4 Conclusion

Our effort to realize a time crystalline phase uses quantum processors in a unique way. The challenge is extrapolating the response to infinite time and system size, which is rooted in the finite coherence time of the system. The core of our work is to devise experimental methods to provide a basis for such extrapolations [4].
